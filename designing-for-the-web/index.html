The best of the two worlds

developers and designers are masters in their filed.

Developers know: limits of technology, cross-compatibility issues, browser capabilities, performance, device limits, semantics and supported features
Designers know: why using a grid rather than another, white space, vertical rhythm, typography, color theory, and so on.

Don't expect to find the unicorn

there are unicorns: developers that became designers, and designers that got into development.
CLOSE> be content with what is at hand and work it out with what you have

Don't expect Tools to solve your problems

designers have approached development with DreamWeaver, GoLive, FrontPage! converting ideas into web pages.
developers have tools that helped get close to design: grid systems, SMACSS, Atomic Design and others.

Don't let anybody decide
which tools you should be using.

Allow yourself to be flexible enough
to experiment and try new things.

Work bottom-up

Work top-down

Bottom-up have devs sit with designers, understand the tools of the trade, elevate devs to focus on frontend design.
Top-down build a use-case, with users in mind, accessibility, usability and device agnosticism.
